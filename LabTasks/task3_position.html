Exploring Modern Web Layouts

Modern web layouts rely on a combination of positioning techniques — fixed, absolute, sticky, and relative — to achieve flexible and interactive designs. These properties give developers full control over how elements behave within the viewport.

As screens get smaller, elements must adjust responsively to provide an optimal experience. Fixed elements may turn static, absolute elements can scale to full width, and sticky components may rearrange into stacked layouts for readability.

Positioning and z-index also play a major role in controlling layer order — for example, keeping headers visible on top of content or ensuring modals overlay everything else.
